About Me

'Oast houses in the Kent countryside' (MelaQuin, Deviant Art)

I was born and grew up in Kent, UK, known as the 'Garden of England' because of its lovely countryside (see picture). In 2018 I gained an MPhys in Physics from Durham University, and I am currently working towards my PhD in the Astronomy Department at the University of Nottingham.

My research mainly focuses on simulations of galaxy clusters. I am particularly interested in cluster dynamics, the evolution of infalling galaxies and groups, and connecting these areas to galaxy evolution and pre-processing. I am involved with The Three Hundred project, a large catalogue of galaxy clusters, simulated with multiple physics models.

Outside of astronomy, I spend my time rock climbing (mostly indoors) and listening to music (mostly from the 1990s), so please talk to me about either of these! I also have an interest in science outreach; I have given talks to the general public, I am a regular contributing author to Astrobites, and I run planetarium shows for children in the Nottingham area through the University of Nottingham's Inflativerse project.
